Day 25: Event Propagation

An interesting demonstration of JavaScript‚Äôs native behaviors. I noticed bubbling is verrrry similar to the way a lightening strike works (more or less‚Ä¶I‚Äôm no meteorologist):

  1. Electricity traces a path through the air from source to destination.
  2. It reaches the destination, followed by a surge launched in the opposite direction.
  3. The surge goes back up the path toward the source.

In JS event propagation, the browser captures events as it seeks its destination node (where the event occurred), starting from the top of the DOM. Then, starting from the destination node, the browser retraces its steps and fires the event listener‚Äôs callback wherever there was an event captured on the way down. It keeps going till it reaches the top of the DOM.

This is really good to know and keep handy for future use.
